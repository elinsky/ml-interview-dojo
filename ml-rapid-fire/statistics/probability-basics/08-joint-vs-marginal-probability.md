# Joint vs Marginal Probability

**Q:** What is the difference between joint and marginal probability?

**A:**

- Joint P(A,B): probability of both A and B
- Marginal P(A): probability of A alone
- P(A) = Î£ P(A,B) over all B (marginalization)
- Joint contains more information
